Location Based Electronic Accessories: Sales Volume and Revenue Analysis

In our comprehensive analysis of product pricing and sales performance, we examined a dataset containing various
products and their respective price points and sales volumes. The insights gathered from this analysis shed light on
the relationship between pricing and sales within the dataset. The mean price across the products in our dataset was
approximately 4,998.838 units of the local currency, while the mean sales volume reached an average of 507.672 units.
These figures provided a general sense of the pricing and sales landscape. Further investigation revealed that the median
price and median sales figures were 3,962.0 units and 520.5 units, respectively. These medians represented the central
tendencies within the dataset, indicating the typical pricing and sales values. Notably, the dataset exhibited a wide range of variation.
The minimum price recorded was 283 units, accompanied by a minimum sales volume of just 4 units. In contrast, the maximum price
reached a significant high of 19,993 units, with the maximum sales volume peaking at 998 units.

One of the noteworthy findings was the correlation coefficient between price and sales, which equated to approximately 0.038.
This relatively low positive correlation suggests that, within this dataset, there was a limited relationship between the pricing of
products and their sales performance. This observation underscores the need for businesses to consider various factors beyond
pricing when optimizing sales strategies.
